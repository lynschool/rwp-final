Language and Religion

More than 90% of the population in Thailand are Buddhist, more specifically Theravada Buddhism. Buddhism is a intergal part of Thailand's culture, and because of that the nation is known as the having the second largest number of Buddhists in the world, behind China. Despite this, Thailand doesn't have any official state religion, nor does it force people to practice any religion. Freedom of religion is a constitutional right in the region, and even the authority does not register new groups that have not been accepted and they limit the number of missionaries in the region. Unregistered religions are free to operate however they like.

The official language of Thailand is, of course, Thai. It is closely related to languages coming from it's neighbors and numerous other smaller languages. 61 other languages are recognised by the government, albiet much, much less spoken. Thai has four different dialects, based on the regions they come from, like Southern and Northern Thai. A large amount of people in the region also speak both Thai Chinese and Thai Lao, although Lao is often confused as Thai.

Ethnic Groups

The majority of people in Thailand are of the Thai and Thai Lao ethnicities, also known as the Central Thai and Northeastern Thai people. They make up 62% of the population. The other groups are the Lanna (Northern Thai), the Dambro (Southern Thai), the Karen (Western Thai), the Thai Chinese, the Khmer, and other much smaller groups.
