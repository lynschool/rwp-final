Yingluck Shinawatra - nicknamed Pou - was both the first female prime minister of Thailand following the 2011 election, and the youngest prime minister Thailand has had in over 60 years.

In May of 2011, the Pheu Thai Party had nominated Yingluck as their candidate in the election. After mass protests happened against her government in 2013, she asked for a dissolution of parliament on December 9th, 2013, which triggered a snap election. On May 7th, 2014 Yingluck was removed from office by the Constitutional Court of Thailand following the months of political crisis.

After being removed from office, the court found her guilty of abusing her power to remove Thawil Pliensri the national security chief, which lead to her arrest along with former cabinet ministers and political leaders who may have helped her. Yingluck fled the country after this, and it is currently unknown where specifically she is, but it is believed to be somewhere in London or Serbia.

Queen Suthida is the current Queen of Thailand, as the fourth wide of King Vajiralongkorn. Suthida was born on June 3rd, 1978, where she then went onto become a flight attendant for JALways in Japan, from 2000 to 2003, later joining Thai Airways International in 2003 until 2008.

Suthida was appointed commander of Vajiralongkorn's houseohld guard in August 2014, with her being romantically linked to the prince as early as 2010 when he was still married to Srirasmi Suwadee. In 2016, the media labeled her as the king's consort, despite the palace never declaring their relationship.

In December 2016, she was appointed to be Commander of the Special Operations Unit of the King's Guard, then being promoted to the rank of general. She reached her rank after only 6 years of service. In May 2019, Suthida was made Queen of Thailand, with the marriage registration taking place at the Amphorn Sathan Residential Hall in Bangkok.

Queen Sirikit is the queen mother of Thailand. She was the Queen of Thailand, and is the mother of the current King of Thailand - Vajiralongkorn. Sirikit was appointed the queen regent in 1956, when the then king had entered a Buddhist monkhood.

Even once the king was done with his period as a Buddhist monk, Queen Sirikit was officially named the Regent of Thailand due to her performing her duties so well. Sirikit was the longest-reigning head of state, as well as the world's longest-serving queen consort.

In 2012, Queen Sirikit started to feel ill, beginning to feel unsteady and staggered while exercising at the Siriraj Hospital, when it was discovered that she had undergone a ischemic stroke - where blood fails to flow properly to the brain, causing cell death. The queen was treated, but has not made any public appearances since. In 2016, she was discharged from the hospital and returned to the Royal Villa.
