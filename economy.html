Recent Economic History

Thailand's economy is largely dependant on exporting goods to other countries. These exports include cars, electrical appliances, rice, fish, rubber, and jewellery. Because of that it has become the second largest economy in Southern Asia and has a GDP of 1.236 trillion American dollars. Most of this growth was recently, starting in the mid-80s but stuttering in the mid-90s. This is when the Asian Financial Crisis started, kneecapping Thailand's economy until it started to recover in 1999.

While it's large exports were enough to keep it growing strong, it was quickly dampened by another financial crisis, this time more local. This forced the government to float the currency and caused the Prime Minister, Chavalit Yongchaiyudh, to resign. Ever since then the economy has continued to grow, yet over recent years has become more and more unstable.

Exports

While China has replaced Thailand as the United States' largest import market, it still holds strong as the third largest. Thailand's biggest customers are Asia and the US and are still one of the world's top car exporters. Manufacturing materials such as machinery, electrical parts, and crude oil still remain as their top exports however.
