History

The area known as Thailand today existed for at least 20,000 years, but the Thai people had only lived there since the 11th century. In 1767, Thailand fought a long war against Burma, but lost to them. It was a complete and utter defeat, where they burnt the capital to the ground and tried to seize control of the entire country. Despite such a significant loss, Thailand quickly got to its feet and kicked the invading army out. The resistance was led by a noble named Taksin, a shrewd but cunning military leader. Within a year he had defeated the Burmese army and restored the kingdom to its former glory, and on 1767 he was crowned King. He is now known today as "Taksin the Great".

In 1917, Thailand joined the First World War on the side of the Allies and gained freedom of taxation and the revocation of extraterritoriality. In 1941, Japan launched an invasion of Thailand, and shortly after Thailand agreed to grant Japan free passage. After WWII, Thailand allied with the United States during the Cold War, and in turn the United States agreed to protect Thailand from communist threats. The support from the United States brought about the modernization and westernization of Thailand, and that made it the country we know today.

Holidays

Thailand uses UTC-7, 11 whole hours ahead of Canada, and doesn't observe daylight saving time. That means they never set their time forwards or back to compensate for the earth's rotation. Thailand celebrates many local and universal holidays, including Makha Bucha, Chakri Day, Songkran, Buddhist Lent, Chulalongkorn Day, and various holidays based on past kings.
