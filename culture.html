Clothing

In Thailand, their traditional clothing is "Chut Thai," which means "Thai outfit" when translated. Men, women, and children can wear it, but it is different for everyone. For women, Chut Thai is usually made up of a Pha Nung or a Chong Kraben, whereas men usually have a Chong Braben or pants, with a Raj pattern shirt and a Sabai. Further North, women may wear a sinh instead of a Pha Nung and a Chong Kraben with either a blouse or a Suea Pat, and for men, a Sado or a Khian Hua.

Other than the Chut Thai, Thailand is primarily a uniform-wearing society, with school uniforms being the norm in Thailand from elementary school up to University. Teachers in these schools will wear uniforms once a week, and civil servants up to the the ministerial level has uniforms they wear regularly.

Cuisine

In Thailand, cuisine falls into roughly four categories. Tom is boiled dishes, Yam is spicy salads, Tam is pounded foods and Gaeng is curries. Pad Thai is likely the most popular dish from Thailand, which is a stir-fry noodle dish commonly served in the streets of Thailand. Pad Thai is commonly made with rice noodles, shrimp, a scrambled egg, bean sprouts, peanuts and various vegetables.

Thai Fried Rice is another popular dish, a variety of mixed fried rice typical of Thai cuisine. It usually consists of rice, a type of meat (various options include chicken, shrimp, or crab as the most common), egg, onions, garlic, and tomatoes. The dish may include seasonings such as soy sauce, sugar, salt, chili sauces, or even nampla, which is then stir-fried together with the other ingredients. The dish is plated and served with various items on the plate.
