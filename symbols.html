National Animal

The national animal of Thailand is the elephant. In Thai culture it represents strength and loyalty. The elephant is both a historic and religious animal, having significant ties with Buddhism, Thailand's most practiced religion. Elephants are still very much used as a symbol in Thailand, showing up on everything from art, clothing, and even beer. They even used to have it on their flag up until the year 1917. One of the biggest buildings in Thailand is the Elephant Building in Bangkok, a huge skyscraper in the shape of, you guessed it, an elephant.

Elephants have been important for a long time in Thailand. From the 1300s to the 1700, specific bridges made of brick were made to withstand the weight of elephants walking across. They were reserved for the royalty (because who else can afford to own an elephant?). They were also built in many religious buildings, as it is a symbol of Buddhism. This is also because elephants are said to be the guardians of Earth, Buddha, and his temples. Buddhists incorporated these into their temples thinking that they'd protect their temples.

National Flower

The national flower, the ratchaphruek, was only recently made the national flower of Thailand (as recent as 2001 can be). While the tree can be found across Thailand, it's actually a fairly common plant all across Southern Asia. It was chosed because it's yellow color is associated with Buddhism.
