Climate

Thailand has a tropical savanna climate for the most part, but has a tropical monsoon climate in the south and the eastern tip. Unlike a lot of countries, Thailand has three different seasons instead of four. The first is monsoon season, which lasts from mid-May to October, with August and September being the rainiest part of the season. Winter lasts from October to February. Winter in Thailand is both dry and mild. Summer, also known as pre-monsoon season, is a warm season. It gets as hot as 30 degrees and is known to be damper than Winter.

Thailand is characterized as being really, really rainy. This is due to maritime influences due to it's proximity to the sea, which causes monsoons to happen in the southern and eastern areas of the country. Monsoons causes the climate to suddenly shift from rainy to dry.

Wildlife

Thailand is home to many different species of tropical wildlife, including bears, gibbons, and their national animal, elephants. Despite being their national animal, poachers have reduced the population from 100,000 to 2,000 in the last 200 years. They are used as livestock and work animals, but the government denies this.
